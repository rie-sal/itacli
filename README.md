**itacli** is a small, stubborn language tutor that lives in your terminal.
It does not have streaks. It does not have gems. It will not send you a push
notification shaped like an owl. It just gives you real reading, real grammar,
and real listening, tracks what you actually struggle with, and quietly makes
you review it until you don't.

It reads the classics so you can, too — then it grabs a Reddit thread when you
want to hear how people really talk. It scrapes, it schedules, it scores, and
when it runs out of things to teach you, it goes and finds more.

*Italian first. The name stays anyway — it's catchy, and we're sentimental.*

---

**what it does**

- Pulls reading from public-domain literature, Wikisource, and native-speaker
  subreddits picked from *your* interests
- Highlight a word you don't know — it's saved, glossed, and headed for review
- One hotkey captures a word from *any* app (yes, even the stubborn ones) and
  turns a sentence into flashcards
- Sends every card straight to Anki, and reads your review stats back
- Grammar, taken seriously — scraped exercises, auto-graded, concept by concept
- Listening from native videos and public-domain film, transcript in hand
- Cloze drills, generated automatically, no cloud required
- A CEFR estimate you can actually watch climb, on a little thermometer
- Adapts the daily mix to your weak spots and the minutes you actually have

**how it thinks**

Deterministic first, clever second. Rules and a local grammar model do most of
the work; a bigger model is invited only when nothing simpler will do. It runs
light, it runs local, and it runs in a terminal — with a pop-up only when a
video genuinely insists.
